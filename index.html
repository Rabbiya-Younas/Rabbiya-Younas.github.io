About

Researcher with expertise in Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning, AI, and data-driven modelling.

I am an experienced researcher with a strong background in Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning and Artificial Intelligence, holding an MS in Information and Communication Engineering from Yeungnam University, South Korea (GPA 4.12, 92%). My work spans hybrid Transformer-based forecasting for cryptocurrencies, state-fusion architectures for MARL, and applied machine learning for real-world problems in NLP, time-series analysis, and computer vision. I have authored multiple conference papers, filed two patents, and am actively working on several journal publications.

Research & Projects

My research focuses on reinforcement learning, deep learning, and data-driven forecasting, with applications in finance, autonomous systems, and intelligent decision-making.

  • Hybrid Transformer-Based Cryptocurrency Forecasting
    Developed a predictive Transformer-based hybrid methodology to investigate the predictability of ten cryptocurrencies at hourly, daily, and yearly frequencies. This work led to a patent on a hybrid attention-correlation-based forecasting framework.
  • State-Fusion Network for Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning
    Introduced a novel state-fusion network as an alternative to traditional mixing networks with self-attention, improving coordination among agents in MARL settings and resulting in a dedicated patent and peer-reviewed publications.
  • NLP and Sentiment Analysis
    Extensive work in text classification, sentiment analysis, named entity recognition, and machine translation. Co-authored “Ac-Net: A Deep Learning Architecture for Enhanced Sentiment Classification of Shopify App Reviews”.
  • Deep Learning for Autonomous Driving Perception
    Applied convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to noisy camera data to improve obstacle detection for autonomous vehicles, focusing on robust perception in challenging conditions.
